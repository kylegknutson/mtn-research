# Access reference — Culebra Range / Cielo Vista Ranch

Quick-reference for the **southern Sangre de Cristo (Culebra Range)** private-access situation, so southern-Sangre planning doesn't re-derive it each time.

**Researched 2026-06-09** across all sources: 14ers.com + listsofjohn.com + peakbagger.com (logged in) **+ climb13ers.com + web** (CFI, Cielo Vista Ranch site, 14ers forum). Note: Cielo Vista Ranch is in the **Culebra Range = southern Sangre de Cristo**, *not* the San Juans — a common mix-up.

## The boundary

The ranch (formerly Taylor Ranch; ~83,000 acres; since 2017 owned by Wm. Harrison / Cathexis Holdings) sits on the **west side of the Culebra crest**. Its **eastern boundary is the crest itself**, running from **De Anza Peak (north) to the NM state line (south)**. From **Maxwell Mountain southward, land on *both* sides of the ridge is private** — stay east of the crest on the public peaks, and trespassing is actively prosecuted.

## Ranked peaks by access

### Public — climbed from the EAST (Blue Lakes / Cuchara, Pike & San Isabel NF) ✅
No ranch dealings; stay east of the crest. (Peakbagger "Land" = *Pike & San Isabel NF* on all four; matches our verified Trinchera-group report.)

- **Trinchera Peak**, **"Leaning South"** (+ Leaning North), **Cuatro Peak**, **Mt Maxwell (PT 13,337)**

### On Cielo Vista Ranch — paid access only 🔒
All climbers register at ranch HQ first. Two sub-trailheads:

| Sub-TH | Ranked peaks |
|---|---|
| **Main ranch HQ / 4WD road** | **Culebra Peak** (14er), **Red Mountain A**, **Vermejo Peak**, **Purgatoire Peak** (PB "Land" = *Private Land*), **"Alamosito"** (= UN 13,466), **PT 13,569** (= UN 13,565), **PT 13,226** (= UN 13,229) (+ Miranda) |
| **Whiskey Pass** (still register at ranch first) | **Francisco Peak**, **Beaubien Peak**, **De Anza Peak B**, **Mariquita Peak** (+ Lomo Liso Mtn) |

> **Mariquita Peak nuance:** its summit is **not actually on CV Ranch property**, but in practice it's reached via the ranch's Whiskey Pass access.

## Fees / policy (volatile — call ahead)

- **$150** for Culebra + Red Mountain.
- **~$500** (reported) for *any other* summit — and access to the non-Culebra/Red peaks is **not guaranteed**; the ranch has required carrying a ranch-supplied **hand radio**.
- Sign-out by 6:00 PM or they treat it as a SAR situation.
- Contact: `cdeleon@cathexis.com` · 719-680-0092 · San Luis, CO. **Verify current policy before planning any peak beyond Culebra/Red.**
