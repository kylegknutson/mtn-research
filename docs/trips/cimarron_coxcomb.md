## Why 2 days, 2 forks

These eight finish Kyle's Coxcomb-area ranked 13ers, but they split cleanly by **trailhead**, not by a single basecamp:

- **The Middle Fork six** sit on the divide **S and NE of Coxcomb** and are done as one big loop from the **Middle Fork Cimarron TH (#227)** — a known full-six outing (4 recorded tracks).
- **Fortress + Precipice** sit **N of Coxcomb** on the **West/Middle Fork divide** and are reached from the **West Fork Cimarron TH** — a steep, loose combo with a deep re-climb between them.

Both forks branch off the same Cimarron road, so the **camp move between days is short** (drop back to the main road, up the other fork). Do **one loop each day**; save the better-weather day for whichever crux you want — both are loose Class 3.

> **One-day attempts are not advised.** These are eight loose, exposed San Juan summits across two drainages; combining them would mean a monster traverse on rotten rock. Two days, two loops, two forks is the sane plan.

## Day plan

### Middle Fork six · ~13.3 mi / ~5,785' · Class 3
The NE trio first (PT 13,222 B → PT 13,340 A → El Punto), then traverse to **Heisshorn** (the loose Class 3 high point), and finish the south pair (PT 13,382 → PT 13,215). **El Punto and Heisshorn are the cruxes** — rotten, exposed rock; the four PTs are Class 2/2+. **Full route, trip reports, and GPX: [Cimarron Remaining Six report](../peaks/cimarron_six.md).**

### West Fork pair — Fortress + Precipice · ~8.7 mi / ~5,202' · Class 3
From the West Fork Cimarron TH: **Fortress → connecting saddle → Precipice** (or each solo). The big gain for modest mileage is the **deep re-climb between them** across deep notches — steep, loose, committing Class 3. (No party has recorded a track walking *between* the two, so the combo connector is composed — see the report.) **Full route + solo options + GPX: [Fortress + Precipice report](../peaks/fortress_precipice.md).**

## Camp

- **Camp low between the forks** (dispersed sites along the Cimarron / fork roads) or at each fork's TH the night before its loop. The relocation between days is a short drive down-and-over, not a long reposition.
- High, exposed basins; water from the fork creeks (treat).

---

## Conditions / season

- **Best window:** **July–September** — high, remote; steep N-facing approaches (especially the Precipice side) hold snow.
- **Terrain:** **loose, rotten Cimarron Class 3** both days — careful scrambling, helmet advised; Heisshorn, El Punto, and the Fortress–Precipice link are the committing bits.
- **Storms:** long exposed divides with few quick bail points mid-loop — very early starts both days.
