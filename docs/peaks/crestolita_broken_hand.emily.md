## Recommended plan — South Colony Lakes ⭐

Both peaks sit above the South Colony basin, reached via **Broken Hand Pass** (~12,900', the standard Crestone Needle saddle).

**Combo stats (from trip-report GPX):** roughly **~11–14 mi and ~6,400–7,100 ft**, depending on how high you drive the 4WD road. A full alpine day, not a half-day.

1. From the South Colony lot, follow the trail toward the lakes / Crestone Needle junction.
2. Ascend to **Broken Hand Pass**.
3. **Broken Hand Peak (13,575', Class 3):** standard line from the pass (NW/west side — *not* the south ridge, which is a hard technical variant).
4. **Crestolita (13,264', Class 2):** the two don't connect at easy class via the direct ridge (that's the 4th–low-5th "Analemma/South Ridge" traverse). For the non-technical pairing, gain Crestolita via its **Class 2 south-facing summit gully**.
5. Return over Broken Hand Pass to the South Colony trail.

> The cleanest *easy-class* way to bag both may be from **Cottonwood Creek** (each peak has a Class 2 south side, skipping the Broken Hand Pass elevation) at the cost of a longer approach.

### Technical alternate 🪨
The Analemma (Crestolita NE Ridge) + South Ridge of Broken Hand linkup is a **5.easy**, extreme-exposure, committing scramble (~12 mi / 5,700') — a premier hard Sangres day, *not* the way to simply tick both. Only for the scramble.

## Conditions / season

- **Best window:** mid-June through September. Snow lingers in the couloirs / N aspects into early summer.
- **Snow option:** Crestolita's north couloirs are a legit spring snow climb (axe/crampons).
- **Rock:** Crestone-group conglomerate — grippy when dry, loose blocks exist; Broken Hand is a true Class 3 scramble.
- **Storms:** standard Sangres afternoon risk on exposed ridge — early start, off by early afternoon.

## Permits / access
- Sangre de Cristo Wilderness — no permits. San Isabel NF — no fee. South Colony Rd: 2WD to the lower lot, high-clearance 4WD for the upper ~2.5 mi.

## Cell coverage
- 14ers community DB: no reports for either summit. Likely dead at the TH/basin; possible signal on the high points (LOS west to the San Luis Valley). **Carry an InReach** — the terrain is committing.
